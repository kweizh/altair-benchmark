### Implementation Summary
The solution is implemented in `/home/user/myproject/chart.py` and generates the self-contained HTML visualization at `/home/user/myproject/chart.html`.

The visualization is constructed as a layered Altair chart utilizing three distinct layers:
1. **Base US States Map**: Renders the geographic background using the US TopoJSON datasets (`data.us_10m.url`) on the `albersUsa` projection.
2. **Flight Routes (Lines)**: Sourced from `data.flights_airport.url`. It performs two coordinate lookups using the airports dataset (`data.airports.url`), one keyed by `origin` (mapping to `latitude` and `longitude`) and one keyed by `destination` (mapping to `latitude2` and `longitude2`). This layer is gated by a `filter` transform referencing the `select_origin` selection parameter.
3. **Airport Circles**: Sourced from `data.airports.url` and rendered at their respective coordinates. A calculated field `origin` is added to map `datum.iata` to the selection's `origin` field. The interactive point selection parameter (`select_origin`) is added to this layer.

### Selection and Interactivity
The interactive behavior uses `alt.selection_point`:
- **Trigger**: Triggers on `click`.
- **Fields**: Keyed on `fields=['origin']`.
- **Default State**: Configured with `empty=False` so that no routes are drawn initially. When an airport is clicked, its originating routes are revealed.
